abstract: >-
  High‑pressure job interviews induce anxiety and impair performance. This study investigates how a robot interviewer’s verbal persona (supportive vs. challenging) and nonverbal backchanneling (active vs. negative) affect users' psychological states and facial expressions in a simulated interview.
  In a 2×2 between‑subjects experiment (N=28), participants interacted with a NAO robot. Subjective measures (state anxiety, self‑efficacy, social presence) and objective facial emotion recognition (DeepFace) were collected.
  Results showed that a supportive persona reduced task load, increased perceived robot warmth and mind perception, and decreased perceived surveillance and robot discomfort.
  Active backchanneling increased non‑neutral (p=0.021) and anxiety‑tension facial affect (p=0.048).
  A significant interaction effect between persona and backchanneling was found on social presence (p=0.045), with congruence amplifying positive experiences and incongruence worsening them.
  Subjective‑objective consistency was 67.9%, supporting multi‑modal assessment. The findings provide preliminary evidence for the social buffering hypothesis in human‑robot interaction.
abstract_zh: >-
  高压工作面试会引发焦虑并损害表现。本研究探讨了机器人面试官的言语人格（支持型 vs. 挑战型）和非言语反馈（积极 vs. 消极）如何影响模拟面试中用户的心理状态与面部表情。
  采用2×2被试间实验设计（N=28），参与者与NAO机器人进行互动。收集了主观量表（状态焦虑、自我效能、社会临场感）和客观面部情绪识别（DeepFace）数据。
  结果显示，支持型人格降低了任务负荷，增加了感知机器人温暖度和心智感知，减少了感知监视和机器人不适感。
  积极反馈增加了非中性（p=0.021）和焦虑-紧张面部情绪（p=0.048）。
  人格与反馈类型在社会临场感上存在显著交互效应（p=0.045）——一致组合放大积极体验，不一致组合则恶化体验。
  主观-客观一致性为67.9%，支持多模态评估方法。研究结果为HRI中的社会缓冲假设提供了初步证据。
